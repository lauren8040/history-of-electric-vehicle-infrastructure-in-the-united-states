History of Electric Vehicle Infrastructure


Electric Vehicles (EVs) have been around for more than 100 years, but they have only recently made a commercial resurgence. Common arguments against EVs have cited the lack of adequate charging stations for taking long-range trips and for those living in urban areas. Our group looked to study these effects by visualizing the placement of different electric vehicle stations, distribution of charging port speeds, car registration patterns, and recent historical events. Using choropleths, line charts, and an interactive timeline, our website helps to unlock relationships within the EV space.

2. Choropleth Map of Electric Vehicle Charging Stations and Ports in USA (1994-2024)


An electric vehicle charging port is a connection point between a charging station and a plugged in electric vehicle . Electric vehicle charging stations are locations where charging ports are housed, with one or more ports at each station. Each port can have a different charging speed, ranging from level 1 (slow charging) to level 3 (fast charging).

Below are two interactive visualizations showing the evolving infrastructure of electric vehicle (EV) charging stations and charging ports in the United States. Explore relationships between population size, location, freeway locations, and charging speeds using the various filters and buttons. The slider can be used to filter stations or ports with an open date on or before the selected year.

Map Controls

Stations

  • Zooming (Two-Finger Pinch or Drag): use zooming to more easily see small counties or the exact locations of EV stations when displayed.
  • Panning: use mouse to click and drag around the screen when zoomed in (does not work for click zoom view only mouse scrolling zoom)
  • Clicking (Single Click on Each State): use clicking to zoom in on a state and only display its data (opacity decreases for rest)
  • Reset Window (Single on the white background or neighboring states): after zooming or clicking on a state, click on the white background or low opacity states to go back to the original map view
  • Year Slider (Play Button or Drag): the year up to and including when the visualized EV stations first opened
  • Display EV Stations (Checkbox): displays the EV stations up to the selected year with green circles. Radio buttons below change the color between red and green with ability to change marker size.
  • Normalize by Population (Checkbox): normalizes the EV station counts by the population of each county * 100,000 people
  • Display Interstates/Freeways (Checkbox): displays the US Freeways built since 2023
  • Display Colors (Checkbox): turns on/off the choropleth color scale
  • Tooltips (hover over counties or EV stations): displays information regarding each county and EV station
